About TIFF to JPG

TIFF (Tagged Image File Format) is the gold standard for high-quality scans and professional photography because it is typically lossless. However, TIFF files are often massive and cannot be viewed in standard web browsers. Converting TIFF to JPG allows you to significantly reduce file size while maintaining excellent visual quality for sharing or web use.

Our converter uses Client-Side Rendering (CSR). Because TIFF files are often large and contain sensitive data, we process everything locally using JavaScript. No photography or scanned documents are ever uploaded to a server, providing you with total security and lightning-fast local performance.

How to Convert TIFF to JPG

  1. Upload: Select your .tiff or .tif files by dragging them into the converter or clicking the upload area.
  2. Queue: Verify your files in the list. You can add several high-res images at once.
  3. Process: Click "CONVERT TO JPG". The tool decodes the TIFF data and renders it into a JPEG format.
  4. Download: Your optimized JPG images will be generated and saved to your device instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it support multi-page TIFFs?

Yes. This converter is designed to detect multi-page TIFF files and will convert the primary page into a high-quality JPG image.

How large of a TIFF can I convert?

Since processing happens on your device, it depends on your computer's memory. Most modern browsers can handle TIFF files up to 100MB without issues.

Is the conversion quality good?

We use high-fidelity decoding to ensure that the colors and details of your original TIFF are preserved as accurately as possible in the JPG output.

Why use client-side conversion for TIFF?

TIFF files are often used for sensitive documents like medical scans or legal papers. Client-side conversion ensures these files never leave your computer, guaranteeing 100% privacy.
