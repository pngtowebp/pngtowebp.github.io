About CR2 to JPG

CR2 (Canon Raw Version 2) is a specialized format used by Canon digital cameras. These files contain uncompressed, unprocessed data directly from the camera's sensor, making them ideal for professional editing but difficult to share or view on standard devices. Converting CR2 to JPG makes your photos instantly accessible for social media, email, and web use.

Our tool operates via Client-Side Rendering (CSR). Raw photo files are often very large and contain sensitive metadata (like GPS location). By processing these files locally in your browser, we eliminate the need for long upload times and ensure your private imagery stays on your computer.

How to Convert CR2 to JPG

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the image quality preserved?

We extract the highest quality image data available in the raw file to ensure your JPEGs look sharp and professional.

How large can the files be?

CR2 files are typically 20MB to 50MB. Since the conversion happens in your browser's RAM, ensure you have enough memory if converting dozens of files at once.

Is the EXIF data kept?

Our converter aims to preserve standard camera metadata like shutter speed, aperture, and ISO in the converted JPG file.

Why convert CR2 to JPG?

JPG files are significantly smaller and universally supported, making them perfect for sharing your Canon photography without requiring special raw-viewing software.
