About JPG to PDF

Our JPG to PDF converter allows you to transform your digital photos and scans into a single, portable document format. PDF is the standard for document sharing, and converting your images ensures that your layout is preserved across all devices and printers.

By using Client-Side Rendering (CSR), we ensure that your private documents never touch our servers. All processing happens locally in your browser's memory, offering the ultimate protection for your data.

How to Convert JPG to PDF

  1. Upload Images: Select one or more JPG files. You can drag and drop them or click the upload area.
  2. Review Order: Your files will appear in the list. The PDF will be generated in the order they were uploaded.
  3. Generate: Click "GENERATE PDF". The tool will process the images and create a single PDF file.
  4. Download: Your browser will automatically prompt you to save the new PDF document.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I combine multiple JPGs into one PDF?

Yes! If you upload multiple JPG files, the tool will automatically place each image on its own page within a single PDF document.

Is there a maximum number of images?

There is no fixed limit, but keep in mind that many high-resolution images will make the resulting PDF file very large and may slow down your browser during generation.

Will my image quality be reduced?

No, we embed the JPGs into the PDF at their original resolution to ensure that the document remains high-quality for printing.

Is this tool secure?

Absolutely. Because the tool works client-side, your images never leave your computer. We do not store or see your files.
