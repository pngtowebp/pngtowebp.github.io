About Excel to PDF

Spreadsheets are essential for data management, but PDF is the preferred format for sharing finalized reports, invoices, and summaries. Converting Excel to PDF ensures that your data layout remains locked, preventing accidental edits and ensuring a consistent look across all devices.

Our tool runs 100% on the Client-Side. By using the browser's ability to render HTML tables and trigger PDF streams, your Excel data is converted locally. This approach guarantees that your financial statements or personal lists are never transmitted to an external server.

How to Convert Excel to PDF

  1. Upload: Click the drop zone or drag your .xlsx/.xls files into the converter.
  2. Queue: Verify the files in the list. You can batch-process several workbooks at once.
  3. Generate: Click "CONVERT TO PDF". The tool will parse the first sheet and prepare the PDF layout.
  4. Download: Use the system print dialog or automatic download to save your PDF files.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my data safe?

Yes. The conversion happens in your browser's memory. Your spreadsheet data is never uploaded to any server or shared with third parties.

Can I convert specific sheets?

This tool currently converts the primary (first) sheet of your Excel workbook. To convert other sheets, move them to the first position in Excel before uploading.

Will my formatting be preserved?

The tool converts cell data and basic styling into a professional table format. Complex Excel-specific themes or macros are not supported in PDF output.

Does it work on mobile?

Yes, modern mobile browsers support this client-side conversion, though large files are best processed on a desktop with more RAM.
