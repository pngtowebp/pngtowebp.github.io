About PDF to Word

PDF files are designed to be "read-only" and look the same on every screen. However, this makes them difficult to edit. Our PDF to Word converter analyzes the text layers within your PDF and reconstructs them into an editable Microsoft Word (.docx) file.

This tool leverages Client-Side Rendering (CSR). Using Mozilla's PDF.js and the DOCX engine, your browser performs the data extraction locally. This means your private contracts, resumes, or financial reports never leave your device.

How to Convert PDF to Word

  1. Upload: Drag your PDF files into the drop zone or click to browse.
  2. Verification: Check the file list to ensure you have the right documents queued.
  3. Conversion: Click "CONVERT TO WORD". The browser will scan the PDF pages for text and images.
  4. Save: The reconstructed editable Word file will be downloaded to your device instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my data secure?

Yes. Because the tool runs entirely in your browser, no PDF data is ever transmitted to our servers. Your documents remain 100% private.

Will the layout stay the same?

We aim for high accuracy. Text and images are extracted in order, though very complex layouts or tables may require minor adjustments in Word.

Can I convert scanned PDFs?

This tool extracts selectable text. If your PDF is a "scanned" image without a text layer, the Word file will contain those images rather than editable text.

Is there a file size limit?

The only limit is your browser's memory. For massive PDFs (100+ pages), a desktop computer is recommended over a mobile device.
